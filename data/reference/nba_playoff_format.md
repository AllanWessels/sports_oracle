# NBA Playoff Format

## Overview

The NBA Playoffs are the annual postseason tournament of the National Basketball Association (NBA).
Sixteen teams — eight from each conference — compete in a single-elimination bracket-style
tournament to determine the NBA Champion. Each series uses a best-of-seven format.

## Regular Season and Seeding

The NBA regular season runs from October to April, with each team playing 82 games.
Teams are ranked within their conference (Eastern and Western) by winning percentage.

### Play-In Tournament (since 2021)

The NBA introduced the **Play-In Tournament** beginning in the 2020–21 season. It determines the
7th, 8th, 9th, and 10th seeds from each conference:

- The **7-seed** and **8-seed** teams play once. The winner becomes the 7th seed in the playoffs.
  The loser gets another chance.
- The **9-seed** and **10-seed** teams play once. The loser is eliminated.
- The winner from the 9-vs-10 game plays the loser from the 7-vs-8 game. The winner of that
  game becomes the 8th seed.

Seeds 1–6 qualify automatically for the playoffs without playing in the Play-In Tournament.

## Bracket Structure

The 16 playoff teams (8 per conference) are seeded 1–8. The bracket is **fixed at the start**
of the playoffs (no re-seeding after each round):

| Matchup | Round 1 |
|---------|---------|
| 1 vs. 8 | First Round |
| 2 vs. 7 | First Round |
| 3 vs. 6 | First Round |
| 4 vs. 5 | First Round |

This applies identically in both the Eastern Conference and Western Conference.

## Playoff Rounds

### First Round (Conference Quarterfinals)

8 series, each best-of-seven. The 16 participating teams are reduced to 8.

### Second Round (Conference Semifinals)

4 series, best-of-seven. 8 remaining teams reduced to 4 (2 per conference).

### Conference Finals

2 series, best-of-seven. Determines the Eastern Conference Champion and Western Conference Champion.

### NBA Finals

The Eastern Conference Champion vs. the Western Conference Champion in a best-of-seven series.
The winner receives the **Larry O'Brien Championship Trophy** and is crowned NBA Champion.

## Home-Court Advantage

The higher seed in each series has **home-court advantage**, meaning they host Games 1, 2, 5
(if needed), and 7 (if needed). The away team hosts Games 3, 4, and 6 (if needed).

The team with home-court advantage hosts the first two games and, if the series extends, the
pivotal Game 7.

## Best-of-Seven Format

Each series is a race to **four wins**. The series ends as soon as one team wins four games.
Possible series lengths: 4 games (sweep), 5, 6, or 7 games.

## Historical Context

- The NBA Playoffs were first held in 1947.
- The number of participating teams has grown from 6 to the current 16 (plus Play-In teams).
- The Finals have used the best-of-seven format since 1985. Prior formats included best-of-five
  for early rounds.
- The Play-In Tournament replaced the traditional 16-team direct qualification in 2021.

## Tiebreaker Rules

If teams finish the regular season tied in winning percentage for a playoff seed, the NBA uses a
series of tiebreakers:
1. Head-to-head record during the regular season.
2. Division winner (if applicable).
3. Record within the division (if same division).
4. Conference record.
5. Record against playoff teams (in their conference, then overall).
6. Net point differential (capped at +/- 3 per game) in division or conference games.
7. Net point differential (capped) in all games.
8. Coin flip.

## Key Records

- **Most NBA Championships:** Boston Celtics and Los Angeles Lakers (tied with 17 each as of 2024)
- **Most consecutive championships:** Chicago Bulls (1991–93, 1996–98) / Minneapolis Lakers (1952–54)
- **Most Finals appearances:** Boston Celtics (22)
- **Most points in a playoff game:** Michael Jordan — 63 points (1986, vs. Boston Celtics)
