# FIFA World Cup Format

## Overview

The FIFA World Cup is the premier international football (soccer) tournament, held every four
years. It brings together national teams from across the globe to compete for the world title.
Since its inaugural edition in 1930, the format has evolved significantly to accommodate a
growing number of national federations.

## Current Format (2026 Onwards): 48 Teams

Starting with the 2026 FIFA World Cup, co-hosted by the United States, Canada, and Mexico, the
tournament expanded to **48 participating teams**, up from 32 teams used between 1998 and 2022.

### Qualification

Each of the six FIFA confederations is allocated a number of qualifying berths, with the
allocation reviewed before each tournament cycle. The host nation(s) qualify automatically.
The remaining 47 spots are distributed roughly in proportion to each confederation's number
of member associations and competitive strength.

### Group Stage (48-Team Format)

- 48 teams are divided into **12 groups of 4 teams** each.
- Each group plays a round-robin (each team plays every other team in the group once).
- Teams earn **3 points for a win**, **1 point for a draw**, and **0 points for a loss**.
- The **top two teams** from each group, plus the **8 best third-placed teams**, advance to the
  Round of 32. This produces 32 teams for the knockout rounds.

Ranking within a group uses: points → goal difference → goals scored → head-to-head record →
disciplinary record → drawing of lots.

### Knockout Rounds (32 Teams to Champion)

| Round | Teams |
|-------|-------|
| Round of 32 | 32 |
| Round of 16 | 16 |
| Quarter-finals | 8 |
| Semi-finals | 4 |
| Third-place playoff | 2 |
| Final | 2 |

In the knockout rounds, matches level after 90 minutes go to **extra time** (two 15-minute
halves). If still level, a **penalty shootout** decides the winner.

## Previous Format (1998–2022): 32 Teams

### Group Stage

- 32 teams split into **8 groups of 4**.
- Round-robin within each group.
- **Top 2 teams** from each group advanced to the Round of 16 (16 teams total).

### Knockout Rounds

| Round | Teams |
|-------|-------|
| Round of 16 | 16 |
| Quarter-finals | 8 |
| Semi-finals | 4 |
| Third-place playoff | 2 |
| Final | 2 |

## Historical Format Notes

- **1930–1938 (13–15 teams):** Groups fed straight into a knockout bracket.
- **1950:** Unique round-robin final group instead of a final match.
- **1954–1974 (16 teams):** Group stage + knockout rounds, with variations in seeding rules.
- **1982–1994 (24 teams):** Added a second group stage or Round of 16.
- **1998–2022 (32 teams):** The format described above, which became the most familiar to modern fans.

## Host Selection

The FIFA Congress votes to award World Cup hosting rights, typically 6–8 years in advance.
Since 2026, joint bids by multiple nations are permitted. Hosts receive automatic qualification.

## Women's World Cup

The FIFA Women's World Cup follows a similar format. From the 2023 edition (Australia/New Zealand),
it expanded to **32 teams** from 24, using the same 8-group, round-robin + knockout structure
that the men's tournament used from 1998 to 2022.

## Key Records

- **Most titles:** Brazil (5 — 1958, 1962, 1970, 1994, 2002)
- **Most appearances in finals:** Germany/West Germany (8)
- **Top scorer (all-time):** Miroslav Klose (Germany) — 16 goals
- **Highest attendance (single match):** ~199,000 — Brazil vs. Uruguay, Maracanã, 1950 final round
